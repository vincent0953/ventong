balanan lake

Balanacan Lake in Negros Oriental is a natural body of water that was formed as a result of a 1925 6.8 magnitude earthquake, which caused a landslide of the Sandulot Mountains and damaged Balanan and Nasig-id ridges. This picturesque tourist attraction is located in Siaton, Negros Oriental, Philippines, offering visitors a mesmerizing experience. The lake is surrounded by hills, tropical rainforest, and century-old dalakit or balete trees, creating a serene and tranquil environment. Balanan Lake provides a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy, including zip-lining, kayaking, pedal-boating, triak, and hiking. If you are seeking a peaceful retreat in nature, Balanan Lake is a must-visit destination in Negros Oriental.

Mansion tower

In Bayawan City, Negros Oriental, the Mansion Tower is a new attraction that offers a stunning city view and overlooks the area. Located at Barangay San Roque, Bayawan City, the Mansion Tower is a refreshing and relaxing place that provides a beautiful view of the city. Visitors can enjoy the scenic beauty of Bayawan from this vantage point. If you are looking for a peaceful and picturesque spot to visit, the Mansion Tower in Bayawan is a must-see destination.

Tricycles, popularly known as “pedicabs, ” is the main transportation mode around town. Fare starts at P15.00, with discounts given to senior citizens, PWDs and students. ABOUT

History

The history of Bayawan dates back to the establishment of a Spanish settlement by friars in 1751. Originally known as Tolong Nuevo, the area existed as early as 1868 as part of Tolong Viejo, which is now the Municipality of Sta. Catalina. It wasn't until 1872 that Tolong Nuevo was formally organized into a pueblo, marking the beginning of Bayawan's official establishment.

History

In the early days, Bayawan was inhabited by indigenous communities, primarily the Buglas people, who lived harmoniously and relied on the abundant resources of the land for their livelihood. Over the years, Bayawan has evolved into a vibrant city with a rich history and cultural heritage.

DELICACIES

Around Town

One of the famous delicacies in Bayawan City is "Baye-baye," a traditional treat that is a local favorite. Baye-baye is made from a ground mixture of toasted glutinous rice and coconut shavings, offering a delightful blend of flavors and textures. This delicacy is unique to Bayawan City and is a must-try for visitors looking to experience the local culinary delights.
